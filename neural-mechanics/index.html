Course Description

Modern AI systems (like large language models) are incredibly powerful but also mysteriously opaque: they work astonishingly well, yet even the engineers who build them don’t fully understand what the billions of artificial “neurons” are actually doing inside. This class focuses on post-hoc interpretability, trying to understand trained models after the fact, like a biologist studying a complex organism. Instead of simplifying the model to make it human-readable, we will probe existing neural networks to uncover what internal structures, features, or "concepts" they’ve learned, and how those parts combine to produce behavior.

Course Goals

The goal of this class is to work towards a research paper production for the Neurips main conference. We will create groups of inter-disciplinary (non CS) PhD students, with PhD students in interpretability to localize LLM concepts that are often overlooked by interpretability researchers.

Pre-requisites

For this class, it would be useful to be familiar with: linear algebra, training ML models, python programming, probability, differential calculus, distributed vector representations, pytorch.
